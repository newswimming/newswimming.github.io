JITP: The Liberatory Legacy of bell hooks: Pedagogies and Praxes that Heal and Disrupt

“This special issue draws on hooks’ radical, inclusive, disruptive, and recuperative legacy to explore the use of digital technology in teaching, educational organizing, and anti-oppressive praxes within, alongside, and beyond academia. We ask: What kinds of embodied and communal interactions are enabled by teaching with technology? How can we reconcile the inherent contradictions in a learning community where technology functions as a tool for social justice and for surveillance capitalism?"

— JITP Editorial Board (Nikki Fragala Barnes, University of Central Florida; Summer L. Hamilton, Pennsylvania State University; Asma Neblett, The Graduate Center, CUNY; Kush Patel, Manipal Academy of Higher Education; Danica Savonick, SUNY Cortland)

This article has been a long time coming, dating back to research during the pandemic, and offers a glimpse into the reservoir of materials I have been forming closer intimacy with. Such is the recursive nature of memory and elusive, somewhat spontaneous, formation of voice. Very grateful for JITP for their invitation to explore the multimodality of language and expression through this special issue. What really has enabled this line of thinking was the opportunity to revisit past work, which has been only possible through the grace of others, a grace rooted in trust; a trust in process, a trust that significant ideas take time, and a trust in carrying each other through its upheaval. To that end, I sigh in both relief and its weight, present two artworks here, and sit patiently, as I continue the untangle, the unravel, the work.
