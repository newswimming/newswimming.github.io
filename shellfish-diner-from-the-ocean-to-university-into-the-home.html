Shellfish Diner, 2024

 

Shellfish Diner is a two-part installation with audio, shells, seaweed, and live dining components made by two sea slugs in Kelptropolis. The first part involved a dining set of ceramic pieces made by co-collaborator Ellie Schmidt in a gallery setting with a 15-min. audio piece, decorated with dried urchin shells with remnant aromas from the sea. This work is Ellie’s creative imagining of Kelptropolis based on a trip we took together to Santa Barbara. In the audio, you hear hydrophone recordings, ambient conversation at the fish market, and encounters with the culture of the harbor. Part two involves a live “story-tasting” performance in which participants are invited to an immersive dining experience at a DTLA home hosted by Jessica Pham and Andrea Kim, two Orange County natives with families in the Asian-American restaurant industry. Andrea prepares dishes from her mother’s favorite childhood snack, deep-fried seaweed, to live urchin, abalone sashimi, and spicy sea pineapple salads inspired by her cultural heritage using ingredients primarily sourced from aquaculture farms. Shellfish Diner is paired with a booklet How to Save the World by Eating Snails, which proposes Kelptropolis as a shared social imaginary and an invitation to consider alternative approaches to LA sea food systems that foreground community and artistic approaches to research.
