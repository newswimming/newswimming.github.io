Trip down Angeleno lane

A Japanese knife is a poetic gift in that the receiver is yielded a tool of certain discretion. The giver is equipping the beloved the means for cutting, stabbing, and of sharpening their skills.

Three palm trees piercing the sky guard the manger of a house. These are the crowns of Arabia and desert land of thick pastoral lore, planted like gates on a Hollywood lawn.

Past the graveyard, and into the castle we go. A group of nine guards one, who hops on his two little feet as he heads toward Aunty. When the soul leaves the spirit. . .

There is a spiral staircase into the courtyard. Sitting, conversing in soft-spoken, curly black metal chaises. Cobblestone clobber, the porcelain of whispers, tip toes, and soft voices.

Blue is the sound of vibrations, especially flickering ones, the rippling of water. You spill out from the pore and feel a shock inside you. Electric, cobalt blue is the warmest color.

Toes sink into wet grass, muddy two feet from gravestones and idle peace. Slowly, slowly, your entire foot is in the ground, as is your leg, then your entire knee. Before you know it, you’ve sunk into the Earth and decomposed into a bag of beliefs, bits and bones, with a dash of celebrity dreams.

Photos taken at Lowell Observatory at Flagstaff, Arizona in October 2022.
