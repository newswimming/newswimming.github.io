About

Andrea [신영, 新泳, xinyoung] is a research scientist and artist based in Los Angeles.

Andrea Kim, M.S. is an artist-scholar examining the fields of ethnocomputing and critical fabulation. Trained in critical theory and the documentary arts, Kim explores traditions of the "real" against theories of virtuality and multiplicity. They have written on reproductive autonomy, digital bodies, and XR, with current interests in designing decentralized language models and data protocols for diasporic arts pedagogy. Kim is a PhD student in the Media Arts + Practice program at the School for Cinematic Arts at USC and an alumni of the MIT Comparative Media Studies Program, MIT Open Documentary Lab and Co-Creation Studio, Duke Center for Documentary Studies, and Duke Literature Program, and has served on selection committees for the Fulbright Association (Visiting Scholars Program) and Peabody Awards (Interactive Media Section). They have also worked previously with CultureHub Los Angeles, MIT Transmedia Storytelling Initiative, LaMama Experimental Theater, U.S. Embassy in Rabat, and the Seoul Institute of the Arts.
