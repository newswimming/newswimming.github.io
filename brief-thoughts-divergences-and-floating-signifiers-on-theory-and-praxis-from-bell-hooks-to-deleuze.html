Brief thoughts, divergences, and floating signifiers on theory and praxis: from bell hooks to deleuze

A few days ago, the professor of our graduate theory seminar at MA+P suggested we respond to our week's readings about theory and praxis through dialogue with a peer. My partner in dialogue, who requested anonymity, is a fellow graduate student in a different department that is more inclined toward literature, and thus who, with great patience, greatly enriched my understanding of language and the fluidity of meanings, inspiring me to draw from and embody deeper archives of knowledge in not only my own work but general way of being. Literature, of course, is not the sole place from which such knowledge is found, and the texts we were responding to were more or less shorter ones: "Intellectuals and Power" dialogue between Deleuze and Foucault, Chapter 1 of "Pedagogy of the Oppressed" by Paulo Freire, and a dialogue between hooks and her writing voice about Friere.

Here is our response. Note that this dialogue, which is not so much a dialogue any more, is positioned within the casual, the playful, and the everyday life as PhD students, with attention to the need for more criticality and concern within these domains.
